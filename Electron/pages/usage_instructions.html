Thanks for downloading Librarian!

Librarian is still in beta, but the most important functionality is stable. Creating documentation without you typing mountains of documents.

Adding documents

There are two ways to add documents to your library:

Use Alt+A to capture a screenshot of the foreground app.

You can try this now by selecting another window, and using the Alt + A keys on your keyboard. Then go to the search view by clicking search icon on the left. Try searching for a keyword that could appear in your screen.

Use Alt+C to capture the contents of the clipboard.

Use ctrl+C to copy text to your clipboard first.

Searching

Librarian supports a wide range of complex queries.

Use '*' to get your most recently added documents.

Search results will only bring up exact matches of your terms by default.
A document containing "The brown fox jumped" will only show up when you type "jumped", but not for "jump"

More advanced users can use "*" for a wider search radius.
Now, the search term "jump*" will bring up the document "The brown fox jumped" in the search results.

Searches by default look for documents containing all the words in the search bar.
If you want to search for documents containing any of the words, add "OR" between your keywords in the search bar.
Ex: "brown OR jumped"

Librarian supports negative keywords. If you want a document to not contain a word add a "-" character in front of the keyword.
Ex: "brown OR jump* -walked"
