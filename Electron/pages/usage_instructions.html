Adding documents

There are two ways to add documents to your library:

Use Alt+A to capture document.
Use Alt+C to capture contents of the clipboard (use ctrl+C to copy text to your clipboard first).

Searching

Librarian supports a wide range of complex queries

Use wildcard '*' to get all your recently added documents.

By default searches will look for full words that appear in a document.
A document containing "Brown fox jumped." will only show up when you enter "jumped".

More advanced users can use "*" for wider search radius.
A document containing "Brown fox jumped." will show up for the search term "jump*".

Searches by default look for documents containing all the words in the search bar.
If you want to search for documents containing any of the words, add "OR" between your keywords in the search bar.
Ex: "brown OR jumped"

Librarian supports negative keywords. If you want a document to not contain a word add a "-" character in front of the keyword.
Ex: "brown OR -walked"
